Protocol Steps

  1. 1

    Preparation of reaction vessel

    ⏱ 10 min Done

    Flame-dry a 25 mL round-bottom flask equipped with a magnetic stir bar under vacuum. Backfill with N₂ gas three times using a Schlenk line. Seal with a rubber septum.

    💡 Ensure all glassware is rigorously dry. Moisture deactivates the Pd catalyst.
  2. 2

    Charge substrates and base

    ⏱ 5 min Done

    Add 4-bromoanisole (187.0 mg, 1.0 mmol), phenylboronic acid (146.3 mg, 1.2 mmol), and K₂CO₃ (414.6 mg, 3.0 mmol) to the reaction flask under N₂ atmosphere.

  3. 3

    Add solvent and catalyst

    ⏱ 5 min Done

    Add DMF (5.0 mL) and H₂O (1.0 mL) via syringe. Add Pd(PPh₃)₄ (57.8 mg, 0.05 mmol, 5 mol%) quickly under N₂ flush.

    ⚠️ Pd(PPh₃)₄ is air-sensitive. Minimize exposure time and work quickly.
  4. 4

    Heat and stir reaction

    ⏱ 12 h In Progress

    Place flask in a pre-heated oil bath at 80 °C. Stir at 400 rpm for 12 hours. Monitor reaction progress by TLC (hexane:EtOAc = 4:1, UV 254 nm).

    Temperature80 °C
    Stir rate400 rpm

    AtmosphereN₂
  5. 5

    Workup — aqueous wash

    ⏱ 30 min Pending

    Cool reaction to room temperature. Dilute with EtOAc (30 mL). Wash sequentially with saturated NH₄Cl (3 × 10 mL), then brine (1 × 10 mL). Dry organic layer over MgSO₄, filter, and concentrate in vacuo.

  6. 6

    Column chromatography purification

    ⏱ 2 h Pending

    Purify crude product by silica gel column chromatography using hexane:EtOAc (9:1) as eluent. Collect fractions, check by TLC, combine pure fractions and evaporate solvent. Record mass of isolated product.
